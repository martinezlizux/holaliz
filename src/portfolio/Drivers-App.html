Optimized Transportation Service: Achieved Significant Cost Reductions through Strategic Design and Research.

Involving users in the design process not only enhances app adoption but also improves its effectiveness.

Keywords Ethnogrphy User Testing Mapping Research
My Role: UX Designer
Timeline: 2 Months
Tools:

About

BD Experience is a leading passenger transfer agency, serving key locations such as Cancun, Punta Cana, Cozumel, and Santo Domingo. They specialize in providing smooth transfers and comprehensive assistance to travelers from around the world.

The company has developed a custom application specifically for their van drivers. This app streamlines transfer operations by allowing drivers to systematically register each assigned service, step by step, ensuring a smooth execution. It also enhances communication between drivers and the logistics team in the office, improving overall coordination and operational efficiency.

Challenge

The lack of effective communication between office staff and drivers, along with the drivers' limited use of the current app, makes it difficult to monitor daily operations. This situation results in a higher volume of phone calls and messages needed for coordination.

Impact

Better tracking of transfers leading to improve communication between departments

Improves app adoption and effectiveness

Reduced technology costs

Consolidated functionalities into a single web application for use on both iOS and Android devices.

Initial discussions with my project manager revealed that no prior research had been conducted on the drivers' use of the app, and they had not been involved in the early design phases. To address this gap, I decided to conduct two days of shadowing and ethnographic research.

Ethnography

This approach enabled me to observe how drivers interacted with the app and identify potential improvements. During this phase, I collected photos, videos, audio recordings, and artifacts to further enrich the research.

5 top findings

High Maintenance Costs.

The use of multiple devices in the process and the persistence of manual tasks led to increased maintenance costs, as the app needed to be implemented on both tablets and cellphones.

Lack of User-Centered Design:

The app was not user-centered; drivers were not involved in the design process, resulting in an app that focused solely on business requirements rather than addressing user needs.

High Volume of Calls

Drivers were not using the app properly, which led to communication issues and a high volume of calls and messages to verify service performance. Additionally, answering calls while driving negatively impacts the service experience and poses safety risks

Process Misunderstandings

Different departments worked in isolation and did not fully understand their impact on the overall process and final service.

Technical and Security Issues

The high security level of the tablet created difficulties in timely action registration, as drivers had to frequently unlock the app to log their activities.

How might we...

1. Reduce Costs by Consolidating to a Single App

The research prompted the decision to redesign the app as a web application, eliminating the need to maintain two separate native apps for different devices. This change will reduce maintenance costs and focus improvement efforts on a single app.

2. Enhance Communication with a Service Dashboard

Design a Service Dashboard that tracks all actions registered by drivers in the app. This dashboard will reduce the volume of calls by providing real-time visibility to everyone involved in the operation.

3. Improve the Driver App and Involve Users in the Design Process

To enhance app usage accuracy, simplify the process for registering actions and involve drivers in the design process. This approach is expected to lead to more accurate and effective use of the app.

4. Clarify Process Understanding

Conduct workshops to analyze the current process and create a service blueprint. This will make the process transparent to all stakeholders and ensure everyone is involved in the design process.

5. Improve the user experience without compromising safety.

Work with security and IT Department to only have the required security locks in the device.

The App

Mockups and First Prototype

I used Figma to design the initial mockups, iterating on the idea with the drivers to create a first prototype for testing.

Test with Users

Five tests were conducted to identify UI improvements before development began. Stakeholders also had the opportunity to preview a more realistic version of the new app. The tests were recorded and shared in real-time with all IT team members, promoting empathy for the users and their needs.

Iterate

Thanks to these initial tests, we achieved the following:

  • Reduced the Number of Screens: Streamlined the interface by identifying and removing non-essential information, minimizing distractions for drivers and enhancing service safety.
  • Minimized Clicks: Simplified navigation by reducing the number of clicks required, preventing overload from processes that interfere with the driver’s primary task.
  • Addressed Security Issues: Communicated to the business the user frustration caused by the dual security lock on tablets, which impeded the registration process.
  • Refined the Proposal: Made improvements to the design based on feedback, preparing a polished proposal for development.

Hand Off

We conducted sessions to implement the task backlog, documenting user stories and acceptance criteria for each task. During these sessions, I worked closely with the development team to review each design requirement and assess its technical feasibility.

Making the research accessible to all team members streamlined the process by ensuring everyone understood the rationale behind the design decisions and the basis for those choices.

The workshop

To improve communication across different departments, I organized a workshop that brought together individuals from various service areas and several developers. The goal of the workshop was to foster collaboration and collaboratively analyze the transfer service flow from the user's perspective.

Take Away

  • Having information and data readily accessible for everyone is essential.
  • Experience alone does not always lead to the best decisions; having data to support your ideas and proposals is crucial.
  • It's common to "evangelize" the role of UX, especially when collaborating with non-technical departments.
  • I was impressed by the insight into the operational steps, which provided the IT team with a clear path toward becoming "paperless." This project also led to initiatives to digitize all operational stationery.
  • I learned that effective communication is vital. For this to happen, the process must be transparent and include those involved in the operations, helping them feel engaged in the company's transformation.
