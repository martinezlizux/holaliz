About

DDI is a global leadership consulting firm recognized as one of the Top 20 Leadership Training Companies by Training Industry. DDI developed an online training platform that provides subscribers with various tools to enhance their leadership performance.

Challenge

Given the ongoing product redesign within the company, the team has recognized the necessity of establishing a design system that provides guidance, serves as a reference and helps to reduce inconsistencies along the flow.

Impact

This initiative streamlined design processes and significantly reduced bugs and inconsistencies in design.

Research

Assessing the current state of the design system

We run a design audit, listing the core components of our interface and the frameworks in use. The team also engaged in discussions about scalability plans and the expected outcomes, which helped us shape the concept of an MVP.

Findings:

  • Lack of maintenance: There had been a previous effort to create a component library in Adobe XD, but due to personnel changes during the pandemic, this library was no longer maintained.
  • Poor documentation: There was no documentation outlining standards and best practices.There was no documentation outlining standards and best practices.
  • Design Process: Designers were working in different teams without a consistent practice of conducting design reviews across the entire team.
  • Duplicated work: Developers were not reusing components.
  • limited resources:The team had limited resources to work on this project.
  • Design time increased: Without a shared component library for the design team, the time required for design increased.

What we needed:

  • A solution that was quick and easy to share with developers.
  • A scalable system.
  • Accessibility as a core requirement.
  • A system that reduced the time needed to create a prototype from scratch
  • A way to minimize inconsistencies between design files and production versions.

Design system fundation

We decided to start by establishing the foundation of our design system and documenting our design standards.

With the knowledge that the company would transition to Figma in two years, we decided to treat this as an MVP and use Adobe XD to establish the foundation of our new design system.

We began by defining variables such as colors, typography, margins, and elevations. Everything that aligned with Atomic Design principles was categorized as an "Atom."

Library in Adobe XD

Using an atomic design system approach, we decided to use two frameworks the company was already using: Bootstrap and Syncfusion, and align these frameworks with our new design tokens.

With a list of key components to cover, we began defining and building master components for our library. Since this was not a primary project on the IT team's roadmap, and we could only dedicate part of our time to it, it took us about six weeks to complete our list of components and document the foundation of our design system MVP.

First goal achieved

We successfully created an updated component library to share among designers, which has reduced the time required for them to create prototypes in Adobe XD.

Components in Storybook

Once we had our components defined in Adobe XD, we started implementing them in Storybook.

A developer from the team helped us establish the structure and set up the repository. We then began adding the components, categorizing them into Atoms, Molecules, and Templates.

During this phase, we worked closely with the developers. The requirement was to use Angular, and they assisted us in adding all the interactions and setting all the properties correctly so that each component could be configured for different states or versions.

When one of the teams started using our library, it allowed us to assess how smoothly the handoff went.

Library of components

Second goal achieved

Having reusable templates helped reduce frontend development time, and using new tokens and variables led to a scalable and robust design system.

Take away

  • To set a realistic scope and minimize team frustration, it’s crucial to recognize that design is as important as maintenance.
  • Effective communication between UX and developers helps reduce bugs and inconsistencies in production.
  • Establishing design standards and properly managing design files—including correctly naming components and grouping elements—is essential.
