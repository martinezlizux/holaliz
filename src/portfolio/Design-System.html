Streamlining Design at DDI: Building a Scalable Design System

My Role: UX Designer
Timeline: Current Project
Tools:
Keywords Design Spec Design System Storybook Handoff Desing Tokens

A global leadership consulting platform identified the need to improve its online training experience to better support its users. As the team initiated a comprehensive product redesign, it became clear that the lack of a unified design system was causing significant challenges. This gap led to inconsistent user experiences and inefficiencies across design and development workflows.

Challenge

The challenge was clear:

Establish a design system that would serve as a single source of truth, guiding designers and developers alike. The goal was to create a cohesive framework that would streamline workflows, reduce redundancies, and ensure a consistent user experience across all touchpoints.

This initiative streamlined design processes and significantly reduced bugs and inconsistencies in design.

Identifying the Challenges

An initial audit of the existing design assets revealed several critical issues. A previously initiated component library in Adobe XD had fallen into disuse due to staff turnover during the pandemic.

Findings:

Finding 1

Lack of maintenance

There had been a previous effort to create a component library in Adobe XD, but due to personnel changes during the pandemic, this library was no longer maintained.

Finding 2

Poor documentation

There was no documentation outlining standards and best practices.There was no documentation outlining standards and best practices.

Finding 3

Design Process no consistent

Designers were working in different teams without a consistent practice of conducting design reviews across the entire team.

Finding 4

Duplicated work

Developers were not reusing components all the time.

Finding 5

Limited resource

The team had limited resources to work on this aside project.

Finding 5

Design time increased

Without a shared component library for the design team, the time required for design increased.

What we needed:

  • A solution that was quick and easy to share with developers.
  • A scalable system.
  • Accessibility as a core requirement.
  • A system that reduced the time needed to create a prototype from scratch
  • A way to minimize inconsistencies between design files and production versions.

Laying the Foundation

Understanding the urgency, the team decided to build a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for the design system using Adobe XD, with plans to transition to Figma in the future.

They began by defining core design elementsâ€”colors, typography, spacing, and elevationsâ€”aligning them with Atomic Design principles. These foundational elements, referred to as "Atoms," served as the building blocks for more complex components.

Over six weeks, they meticulously created and documented master components, despite the project not being a primary focus on the IT roadmap. This foundational work was crucial in setting the stage for a more efficient and cohesive design process.

Building the Component Library

With the foundational elements in place, the next step was to develop a comprehensive component library.

The team focused on creating reusable components that could be easily shared among designers, significantly reducing the time required to build prototypes in Adobe XD.

This library not only streamlined the design process but also ensured consistency across different projects and teams.

Recognizing the importance of collaboration between design and development, I implemented the components into Storybook. A developer assisted me in setting up the repository, categorizing components into Atoms, Molecules, and Templates. Developer added necessary interactions and properties, allowing each component to be configured for various states or versions. This collaborative effort ensured that the components were both design-friendly and developer-ready.

Achieving Tangible Results

The success of the design system was further validated when one of the teams began using the library. The smooth handoff and integration demonstrated the system's effectiveness in bridging the gap between design and development. Reusable templates contributed to reduced frontend development time, and the use of new tokens and variables ensured consistency and scalability across the platform.
