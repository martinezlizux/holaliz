Understanding the Traveler’s Mindset: A UX Journey into Online Hotel Bookings

My Role: UX Designer
Timeline: 1 month
Tools:
Keywords Research How might we... Ideation

As a UX Designer for a leading OTA (Online Travel Agency) in Latam, I was tasked with conducting user research and gathering data to gain insights into the online hotel booking process and identify the pain points users encounter

Challenge

The Challenge

While our analytics data showed us what users were doing, it didn’t tell us why. We needed to go beyond the numbers to deeply understand the behavior, motivations, and frustrations of travelers as they searched for and booked accommodations online.

Goal

My Goal

My goal was to thoroughly understand the entire booking process and provide the team with data that would guide better decision-making for future features on our platform.

The Research Journey

By getting closer to our clients, we can better understand the key factors that influence their decisions when planning a trip and booking online.

To design a better product, it's essential to understand how users search and make decisions.

Strategic Planning

I began by creating a roadmap to outline the timeline for the entire research process, from recruiting participants to delivering a report with the insights gathered.

Strategic Planning

I designed a complete research roadmap—from defining objectives and recruiting participants to synthesizing insights and delivering a stakeholder-ready report.

The Right Tools for the Job

I used Ethnio for recruiting and Lookback to conduct contextual interviews remotely. These tools allowed us to create a seamless and natural experience for participants.

Talking to Real Users

I interviewed 18 users between the ages of 21 and 39—men and women who had booked hotels online in the past 6 months. These conversations gave us rich, qualitative data about their habits and expectations.

Collaborative Synthesis

All interviews were transcribed, tagged, and visualized in Miro, allowing us to identify patterns and key themes quickly. We combined these findings with Google Analytics data to add depth and context.

Delivering Impact

I created a prioritized report highlighting major pain points, behavioral patterns, and missed opportunities—especially around features like map-based search, which users wanted but struggled to use.

Key Outcomes

One of the most impactful discoveries was the friction users experienced with map search when trying to find lodging in specific neighborhoods.

By highlighting this, we were able to propose UX improvements that directly addressed user needs.

Other notable outcomes included:

Outcome 1

🌟 Improve our tracking

Identifying key actions that weren’t being tracked in Google Analytics, which we added to improve data accuracy.

Outcome 2

🌟 Refine our backlog

Prioritizing research-backed design tasks in our backlog for the next development sprint.

Outcome 3

🌟 Data-Driven actions

Aligning cross-functional teams around real user needs—not assumptions.

One key finding led us to further test our search-by-map feature, resulting in an improved user experience for those searching for hotels on a map

Take Away

The research process taught me several valuable lessons about conducting remote interviews:

  • Have a Backup Plan: Not all users attend their scheduled sessions, even if they confirm a day in advance. Ethnio feature to schedule two users simultaneously was helpful, as it allowed one of the researchers to conduct the session if both users showed up.
  • Ensure Proper Setup:Make sure users receive clear instructions for installing any required programs for the session.
  • Prioritize Transcription:Transcribing interviews took longer than anticipated, but it's a crucial and time-consuming task. Accurate transcripts are essential to avoid unreliable data.
  • Share Findings with the Team: Ensure the entire team has access to the research process and findings. It's important for everyone to understand and empathize with the users' issues.
